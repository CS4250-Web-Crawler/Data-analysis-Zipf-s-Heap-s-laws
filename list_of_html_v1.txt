         <td id="mp-left" class="MainPageBG mp-bordered"> <h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
          <div id="mp-tfa">
           <div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
            <div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 162px;"> <a href="/wiki/File:Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg" class="image" title="Burmese refugees in Thailand's Mae La camp, which works with UNHCR"><img alt="Burmese refugees in Thailand's Mae La camp, which works with UNHCR" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg/162px-Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg" decoding="async" width="162" height="122" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg/243px-Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg/324px-Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg 2x" data-file-width="672" data-file-height="504"></a>
             <div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">
              Burmese refugees in Thailand's <a href="/wiki/Mae_La_refugee_camp" title="Mae La refugee camp">Mae La camp</a>, which works with <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_High_Commissioner_for_Refugees" title="United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees">UNHCR</a>

            </div>
           </div>
           <p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Gateway_Protection_Programme" title="Gateway Protection Programme">Gateway Protection Programme</a></b> was operated by the British government between 2004 and 2020, in partnership with the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_High_Commissioner_for_Refugees" title="United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees">United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees</a> (UNHCR) and co-funded by the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, offering a legal route for a quota of UNHCR-identified refugees to settle in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. It was proposed by the British Home Secretary, <a href="/wiki/David_Blunkett" title="David Blunkett">David Blunkett</a>, in October 2001, and its legal basis was established by the <a href="/wiki/Nationality,_Immigration_and_Asylum_Act_2002" title="Nationality, Immigration and Asylum Act 2002">Nationality, Immigration and Asylum Act 2002</a>. The programme launched in March&nbsp;2004, initially with a quota of 500 and later 750 refugees per year, but the actual number of refugees resettled in most years was fewer than the quota permitted. Evaluations of the programme have praised it but noted problems in securing employment for refugees. In March&nbsp;2020, the Gateway Protection Programme closed after resettling 9,939 refugees, including <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_the_Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Demographics of the Democratic Republic of the Congo">Congolese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Somalia" title="Demographics of Somalia">Somali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Iraq" title="Demographics of Iraq">Iraqi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Ethiopia" title="Demographics of Ethiopia">Ethiopian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Sudan" title="Demographics of Sudan">Sudanese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Myanmar" title="Demographics of Myanmar">Burmese</a> nationals. A new UK Resettlement Scheme started in February&nbsp;2021. (<b><a href="/wiki/Gateway_Protection_Programme" title="Gateway Protection Programme">Full&nbsp;article...</a></b>) </p>

          <div id="mp-otd">
           <p><b><a href="/wiki/October_1" title="October 1">October 1</a></b>: <a href="/wiki/Unification_Day_(Cameroon)" title="Unification Day (Cameroon)">Unification Day</a> in <b><a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/1961" title="1961">1961</a>); <a href="/wiki/List_of_national_independence_days" title="List of national independence days">Independence Day</a> in <b><a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Nigeria)" title="Independence Day (Nigeria)">Nigeria</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/1960" title="1960">1960</a>) and <b><a href="/wiki/Tuvalu" title="Tuvalu">Tuvalu</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/1978" title="1978">1978</a>); <b><a href="/wiki/Filipino_American_History_Month" title="Filipino American History Month">Filipino American History Month</a></b> begins </p>
           <div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;" id="mp-otd-img">
            <div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 152px;"> <a href="/wiki/File:St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg" class="image" title="St Pancras railway station"><img alt="St Pancras railway station" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg/152px-St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg" decoding="async" width="152" height="128" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg/228px-St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg/304px-St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg 2x" data-file-width="8466" data-file-height="7140"></a>
             <div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">
              St Pancras railway station
             </div>

           </div>
           <ul>

        <h2 id="mp-tfl-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_list"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_list">From today's featured list</span></h2>

          <div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 118px;"> <a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg" class="image" title="Michael Whelan"><img alt="Michael Whelan" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg/118px-Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="118" height="166" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg/177px-Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg/236px-Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1382" data-file-height="1941"></a>

         <p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Hugo_Award_for_Best_Professional_Artist" title="Hugo Award for Best Professional Artist">Hugo Award for Best Professional Artist</a></b> is given each year for artists of works related to science fiction or fantasy released in the previous calendar year. The award has been given annually under several names since 1955, with the exception of 1957. The <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Award" title="Hugo Award">Hugo Awards</a> have been described as "a fine showcase for speculative fiction" and "the best known literary award for science fiction writing". The inaugural 1953 Hugo Awards recognized "Best Interior Illustrator" and "Best Cover Artist" categories, awarded to <a href="/wiki/Virgil_Finlay" title="Virgil Finlay">Virgil Finlay</a> and a tie between <a href="/wiki/Hannes_Bok" title="Hannes Bok">Hannes Bok</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ed_Emshwiller" title="Ed Emshwiller">Ed Emshwiller</a>, respectively. The Best Professional Artist award was simply named "Best Artist" in 1955 and 1956, was not awarded in 1957, and was named "Outstanding Artist" in 1958, finally changing to its current name the following year. During the 74 nomination years, 89 artists have been nominated; 24 of these have won, including co-winners and Retrospective Hugo Awards. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Whelan" title="Michael Whelan">Michael Whelan</a> <i>(pictured)</i> has received the most awards, with 13 wins out of 24 nominations. <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Freas" title="Kelly Freas">Frank Kelly Freas</a> has 11 wins and 28 nominations, the most nominations of any artist. <i>(<b><a href="/wiki/Hugo_Award_for_Best_Professional_Artist" title="Hugo Award for Best Professional Artist">This&nbsp;list</a></b> is part of a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_topics" title="Wikipedia:Featured topics">featured topic</a>: </i><b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_topics/Hugo_Awards" title="Wikipedia:Featured topics/Hugo Awards"><i>Hugo Awards</i></a></b><i>.)</i> </p>

         <td id="mp-left" class="MainPageBG mp-bordered"> <h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
          <div id="mp-tfa">
           <div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
            <div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 162px;"> <a href="/wiki/File:Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg" class="image" title="Burmese refugees in Thailand's Mae La camp, which works with UNHCR"><img alt="Burmese refugees in Thailand's Mae La camp, which works with UNHCR" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg/162px-Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg" decoding="async" width="162" height="122" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg/243px-Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg/324px-Mae_La_refugee_camp_TFA.jpg 2x" data-file-width="672" data-file-height="504"></a>
             <div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">
              Burmese refugees in Thailand's <a href="/wiki/Mae_La_refugee_camp" title="Mae La refugee camp">Mae La camp</a>, which works with <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_High_Commissioner_for_Refugees" title="United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees">UNHCR</a>

            </div>
           </div>
           <p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Gateway_Protection_Programme" title="Gateway Protection Programme">Gateway Protection Programme</a></b> was operated by the British government between 2004 and 2020, in partnership with the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_High_Commissioner_for_Refugees" title="United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees">United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees</a> (UNHCR) and co-funded by the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, offering a legal route for a quota of UNHCR-identified refugees to settle in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. It was proposed by the British Home Secretary, <a href="/wiki/David_Blunkett" title="David Blunkett">David Blunkett</a>, in October 2001, and its legal basis was established by the <a href="/wiki/Nationality,_Immigration_and_Asylum_Act_2002" title="Nationality, Immigration and Asylum Act 2002">Nationality, Immigration and Asylum Act 2002</a>. The programme launched in March&nbsp;2004, initially with a quota of 500 and later 750 refugees per year, but the actual number of refugees resettled in most years was fewer than the quota permitted. Evaluations of the programme have praised it but noted problems in securing employment for refugees. In March&nbsp;2020, the Gateway Protection Programme closed after resettling 9,939 refugees, including <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_the_Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Demographics of the Democratic Republic of the Congo">Congolese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Somalia" title="Demographics of Somalia">Somali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Iraq" title="Demographics of Iraq">Iraqi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Ethiopia" title="Demographics of Ethiopia">Ethiopian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Sudan" title="Demographics of Sudan">Sudanese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Myanmar" title="Demographics of Myanmar">Burmese</a> nationals. A new UK Resettlement Scheme started in February&nbsp;2021. (<b><a href="/wiki/Gateway_Protection_Programme" title="Gateway Protection Programme">Full&nbsp;article...</a></b>) </p>

          <div id="mp-otd">
           <p><b><a href="/wiki/October_1" title="October 1">October 1</a></b>: <a href="/wiki/Unification_Day_(Cameroon)" title="Unification Day (Cameroon)">Unification Day</a> in <b><a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/1961" title="1961">1961</a>); <a href="/wiki/List_of_national_independence_days" title="List of national independence days">Independence Day</a> in <b><a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Nigeria)" title="Independence Day (Nigeria)">Nigeria</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/1960" title="1960">1960</a>) and <b><a href="/wiki/Tuvalu" title="Tuvalu">Tuvalu</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/1978" title="1978">1978</a>); <b><a href="/wiki/Filipino_American_History_Month" title="Filipino American History Month">Filipino American History Month</a></b> begins </p>
           <div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;" id="mp-otd-img">
            <div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 152px;"> <a href="/wiki/File:St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg" class="image" title="St Pancras railway station"><img alt="St Pancras railway station" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg/152px-St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg" decoding="async" width="152" height="128" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg/228px-St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg/304px-St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg 2x" data-file-width="8466" data-file-height="7140"></a>
             <div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">
              St Pancras railway station
             </div>

           </div>
           <ul>

        <h2 id="mp-tfl-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_list"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_list">From today's featured list</span></h2>

          <div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 118px;"> <a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg" class="image" title="Michael Whelan"><img alt="Michael Whelan" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg/118px-Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="118" height="166" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg/177px-Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg/236px-Michael_Whelan_-_Lucca_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1382" data-file-height="1941"></a>

         <p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Hugo_Award_for_Best_Professional_Artist" title="Hugo Award for Best Professional Artist">Hugo Award for Best Professional Artist</a></b> is given each year for artists of works related to science fiction or fantasy released in the previous calendar year. The award has been given annually under several names since 1955, with the exception of 1957. The <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Award" title="Hugo Award">Hugo Awards</a> have been described as "a fine showcase for speculative fiction" and "the best known literary award for science fiction writing". The inaugural 1953 Hugo Awards recognized "Best Interior Illustrator" and "Best Cover Artist" categories, awarded to <a href="/wiki/Virgil_Finlay" title="Virgil Finlay">Virgil Finlay</a> and a tie between <a href="/wiki/Hannes_Bok" title="Hannes Bok">Hannes Bok</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ed_Emshwiller" title="Ed Emshwiller">Ed Emshwiller</a>, respectively. The Best Professional Artist award was simply named "Best Artist" in 1955 and 1956, was not awarded in 1957, and was named "Outstanding Artist" in 1958, finally changing to its current name the following year. During the 74 nomination years, 89 artists have been nominated; 24 of these have won, including co-winners and Retrospective Hugo Awards. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Whelan" title="Michael Whelan">Michael Whelan</a> <i>(pictured)</i> has received the most awards, with 13 wins out of 24 nominations. <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Freas" title="Kelly Freas">Frank Kelly Freas</a> has 11 wins and 28 nominations, the most nominations of any artist. <i>(<b><a href="/wiki/Hugo_Award_for_Best_Professional_Artist" title="Hugo Award for Best Professional Artist">This&nbsp;list</a></b> is part of a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_topics" title="Wikipedia:Featured topics">featured topic</a>: </i><b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_topics/Hugo_Awards" title="Wikipedia:Featured topics/Hugo Awards"><i>Hugo Awards</i></a></b><i>.)</i> </p>
